Bellabeat

"Health & wellness"

Bella Beat

Bellabeat is a successful small company, but they have the potential to become a larger player in the global smart device market. Urška Sršen and Sando Mur founded Bellabeat, a high-tech company that manufactures health-focused smart products. Sršen used her background as an artist to develop beautifully designed technology that informs and inspires women around the world. Collecting data on activity, sleep, stress, and reproductive health has allowed Bellabeat to empower women with knowledge about their own health and habits. Since it was founded in 2013, Bellabeat has grown rapidly and quickly positioned itself as a tech-driven wellness company for women.

Products

  • apps App

    The app provides health data related to their activity, sleep, stress, menstrual cycle, and habits. Understand their current habits, make healthy decisions, and connect to their line of smart wellness products.

  • diamond Leaf

    Bellabeat’s classic wellness tracker can be worn as a bracelet, necklace, or clip. The Leaf tracker connects to the Bellabeat app to track activity, sleep, and stress.

  • schedule Time

    This wellness watch combines the timeless look of a classic timepiece with smart technology to track user activity, sleep, and stress. The Time watch connects to the Bellabeat app to provide you with insights into your daily wellness.

  • water_bottle Spring

    This is a water bottle that tracks daily water intake using smart technology to ensure that you are appropriately hydrated throughout the day. The Spring bottle connects to the Bellabeat app to track your hydration levels.

  • loyalty Membership

    Bellabeat also offers a subscription-based membership program for users. Membership gives users 24/7 access to fully personalized guidance on nutrition, activity, sleep, health and beauty, and mindfulness based on their lifestyle and goals.

Purpose

Urška Sršen, cofounder and Chief Creative Officer of Bellabeat, believes that analyzing smart device fitness data could help unlock new growth opportunities for the company. You have been asked to focus on one of Bellabeat’s products and analyze smart device data to gain insight into how consumers are using their smart devices. The insights you discover will then help guide marketing strategy for the company. The project will be presented to the Bellabeat executive team along with high-level recommendations for Bellabeat’s marketing strategy.

Sršen knows that an analysis of Bellabeat’s available consumer data would reveal more opportunities for growth. She has asked the marketing analytics team to focus on a Bellabeat product and analyze smart device usage data in order to gain insight into how people are already using their smart devices. Then, using this information, she would like high-level recommendations for how these trends can inform Bellabeat marketing strategy

Daily Travelling Analyze

Based on the two graph, we can see that:

  • Customers travelled the most on Tuesday and Saturday
  • Customers have less frequency of travelling on Sunday and Thursday comparing with others
  • As we can imagine, there is a correlation between the steps and the distance that customers travelled. The more their steps, the further they go.

Hourly Calories Consumption Analyze

Based on the line chart, we can see that:

  • From 5am to 7pm, the period has the uptrend of calories consumption.
  • From 12pm to 2pm, and from 5pm to 7pm, these are the highest calories consumption period
  • The trend significantly decrease since 8pm to 4am. In other words, during these time, our customer relaxes and do the least exercise or they are sleeping

Calories versus Steps Analyze

Based on the scatter plot and the line chart, we can see that:

  • There is a trend between steps and calories, the higher the steps, the higher calories consumption.
  • There is a big density of the dots ranging between 2000 calories to 3000 calories.
  • There is also an indication near as we see there are scatter plot in intersection of 0 and 0, which might mean the customers didn't wear the device.
  • As there are some records that having calories over 3000 per day, we can assume that these might be athletes' user, which we can add to the demographic marketing strategy.
